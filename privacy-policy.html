Our Commitment

Go Outside Foundation ("the Foundation," "we," "us," or "our") is committed to protecting the privacy of our participants, donors, volunteers, and website visitors.

This Privacy Policy describes how we collect, use, and share information through our website, our program registration processes, and our interactions with partner institutions.

By using our website or participating in our programs, you agree to the practices described in this Privacy Policy.

1. Information We Collect

1.1 Information You Provide to Us

We collect information that you voluntarily provide when you register for programs, sign up for our newsletter, make a donation, or contact us. This may include:

  • Contact Information: Name, email address, mailing address, and phone number.
  • Participant Data: For youth programs, we collect student names, ages, grades, school affiliations, and emergency contact information.
  • Donor Information: Name, billing address, and transaction details. We do not store full credit card numbers; these are processed by secure third-party payment processors.
  • Volunteer & Staff Data: Information provided for background checks, including identifiers required for compliance with school district safety protocols.

1.2 Automatically Collected Information

When you visit our website, we may automatically collect certain technical information, including your IP address, browser type, operating system, and data regarding your interaction with our site, such as pages viewed and links clicked, to improve our digital experience.

2. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • Program Administration: To register participants for GO Mode, The W.A.Y. Club, and other initiatives, and to communicate program details.
  • Safety and Compliance: To conduct required background checks and ensure the safety of all participants in accordance with partner school district requirements.
  • Reporting and Evaluation: To track program attendance and outcomes, often required for grant reporting and school district data systems such as Aspen.
  • Communication: To send updates, newsletters, and information about upcoming events or fundraising campaigns.
  • Donorship: To process donations, issue tax receipts, and maintain donor records.

3. How We Share Your Information

3.1 With Partners and Service Providers

We may share necessary information with trusted partners to facilitate our programs:

  • Educational Partners: We share student participation data with Chicago Public Schools (CPS) and other partner districts as required by contract or for safety purposes.
  • Logistics Partners: We may share participant names with transportation providers or venue coordinators, such as museums or tour operators, strictly for manifest and safety requirements.
  • Food Service Vendors: Necessary dietary or allergy information may be shared with meal providers, such as Gourmet Gorilla, to ensure participant health.
  • Payment Processors: Donor information is shared with secure third-party processors to complete financial transactions.

3.2 Legal and Regulatory Requirements

We may disclose information if required to do so by law, such as in response to a court order, subpoena, or to comply with the Illinois Student Online Personal Protection Act (SOPPA) and the Personal Information Protection Act (PIPA).

3.3 We Do Not Sell Your Data

Go Outside Foundation does not sell, rent, or trade your personal information or our mailing lists with third parties for marketing purposes.

4. Children’s Privacy and Student Data

The Foundation is committed to the privacy of minors.

  • COPPA Compliance: We do not knowingly collect personal information from children under 13 without verifiable parental or guardian consent.
  • Illinois SOPPA Compliance: For programs operating within Illinois schools, we handle student data as a covered entity or operator under the Student Online Personal Protection Act (SOPPA). We implement administrative, physical, and technical safeguards to protect covered information and will notify partner districts in the event of a documented data breach as required by law.

5. Data Security

We implement industry-standard security measures, including encryption and secure server hosting, to protect against the loss, misuse, or alteration of the information under our control. However, no data transmission over the internet can be guaranteed to be 100% secure.

6. Your Rights and Choices

  • Access and Correction: You may request to review or update your personal information by contacting us.
  • Opt-Out: You may opt out of receiving promotional emails by following the unsubscribe link in the email or contacting us directly.
  • Data Deletion: You may request the deletion of your personal data, subject to legal, tax, or school district record-retention requirements.

7. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy periodically. We will notify you of material changes by posting the new policy on our website and updating the Effective Date at the top.

8. Contact Us

If you have questions or concerns about this Privacy Policy, please contact:
